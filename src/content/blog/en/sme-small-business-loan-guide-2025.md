### 🥇 #1: Government SME Financing Schemes

**Best for: Startups & growing SMEs yang nak low-cost financing**

#### A. **Tekun Nasional** - Untuk Bumiputera Entrepreneurs

Tenure: Up to 5 years
Grace Period: 3 months
Collateral: Not required
```

```
Loan Amount: Up to RM200,000
Interest Rate: 4% per annum
Tenure: Up to 7 years
Grace Period: 6 months
Target: High potential entrepreneurs
```

**3. Tekun Enterprise**
```
Loan Amount: RM50,000 - RM500,000
Interest Rate: 4% per annum
Tenure: Up to 10 years
Grace Period: 6-12 months
Target: Established SMEs for expansion

- ✓ Warganegara Malaysia (Bumiputera Muslim)

##### 🎯 Sesuai Untuk:
- Bumiputera Muslim entrepreneurs
- Startup dengan business plan yang kuat

### #4: Islamic Business Financing (Shariah-Compliant)

**For businesses yang prefer Shariah-compliant financing**

#### 📋 Main Products:

##### **A. Murabahah (Cost-Plus Financing)**
```
Concept: Bank beli asset untuk business, jual dengan markup
Amount: Varies by bank
Rate: Profit rate (similar to conventional interest)
Tenure: Up to 10 years
Best for: Asset purchase (machinery, vehicles, equipment)
```

##### **B. Musharakah (Partnership)**
```
Concept: Bank jadi partner dalam business
Amount: Varies by bank
Profit sharing: Agreed ratio (e.g., 70:30)
Tenure: Flexible
Best for: Joint venture projects, expansion
```

##### **C. Mudarabah (Profit-Sharing)**
```
Concept: Bank provide capital, you provide expertise/labor
Amount: Varies
Profit sharing: Agreed ratio
Loss: Bank tanggung capital loss, you tanggung effort
Best for: Trading businesses, ventures
```

#### 🏦 Available At:

- Bank Islam Malaysia
- Maybank Islamic
- CIMB Islamic
- RHB Islamic
- Hong Leong Islamic
- Public Bank Islamic

#### ✅ Requirements:

Similar to conventional loans PLUS:
- ✓ Business activity mesti halal
- ✓ Cannot be involved in:
  - Alcohol production/sales
  - Gambling
  - Conventional banking
  - Tobacco
  - Pork-related
  - Other non-Shariah industries

#### 🎯 Sesuai Untuk:
- Muslim entrepreneurs yang prefer Shariah financing
- Halal businesses
- Those seeking profit-sharing models

### #6: Invoice Financing / Trade Financing

**Convert unpaid invoices into immediate cash**

#### 💡 How It Works:

```
You have invoice RM50,000 (payment dalam 60 hari)
     ↓
Apply invoice financing
     ↓
Get 80-90% immediately (RM40,000-RM45,000)
     ↓
Customer bayar RM50,000 selepas 60 hari
     ↓
You terima balance minus fees
```

#### 📋 Types:

##### **A. Invoice Factoring**
- Lender beli invoices from you
- They collect payment dari customer
- You get 80-90% upfront
- Get balance after customer pays (minus fees)

##### **B. Invoice Discounting**
- You retain control of collections
- Lender advance 80-90% of invoice value
- Customer bayar you directly
- You bayar lender + fees

#### 💸 Typical Terms:

```
Advance Rate: 70-90% of invoice value
Fees: 2-5% of invoice value OR monthly interest 1-2%
Tenure: Based on invoice payment terms (30-90 days)
Minimum Invoice Value: RM10,000-RM50,000
```

#### 🏦 Providers:

- Major banks (Maybank, CIMB, RHB, etc.)
- P2P platforms (Funding Societies, Versa)
- Specialized trade finance companies

#### 🎯 Sesuai Untuk:
- B2B businesses dengan corporate clients
- Government contractors
- Businesses dengan long payment terms
- Need improve cash flow
- Seasonal businesses

## ⚡ 7 Langkah Apply SME Loan (Step-by-Step)

Ikut process ini untuk maximum success rate:

### **Step 1: Assess Business Needs & Kemampuan Bayar** ⏱️ 1-2 hari

#### 🎯 Tentukan:

**A. Berapa Sebenarnya Perlu?**
```
Don't just guess - Calculate properly:

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
CONTOH: F&B Business Nak Expand
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Kitchen equipment: RM80,000
Renovation: RM50,000
Initial inventory: RM30,000
Working capital (3 months): RM45,000
Marketing & promotion: RM15,000
Contingency (10%): RM22,000
─────────────────────────────────────
TOTAL NEEDED: RM242,000
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

**B. Boleh Bayar Berapa Monthly?**

**Formula**:
```
Monthly business revenue: RM[A]
Minus COGS (40-60%): RM[B]
Minus operating expenses: RM[C]
Minus owner/staff salary: RM[D]
───────────────────────────────────
Available for loan: RM[E]

Safe loan payment: 40-50% of [E]
```

**Example**:
```
Revenue: RM100,000
COGS (50%): RM50,000
Operating: RM25,000
Salaries: RM15,000
───────────────────
Available: RM10,000

Safe loan payment: RM4,000-RM5,000/month
```

**C. Berapa Tenure Yang Sesuai?**

```
If boleh bayar RM5,000/month:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Loan RM100K at 6% interest:
- 24 months = RM4,437/month
- 36 months = RM3,042/month ✓ (more comfortable)
- 48 months = RM2,349/month ✓ (most comfortable)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

**💡 Rule of Thumb**: Choose tenure that keeps monthly payment at 40-50% of available cash flow untuk maintain buffer.

### **Step 2: Choose Right Type of Financing** ⏱️ 2-3 hari

**Decision Tree**:

```
START: What do you need loan for?
  │
  ├─ Working capital (daily operations)
  │   ├─ Revenue <RM100K/month → Microfinance / Licensed ML
  │   ├─ Have unpaid invoices → Invoice Financing
  │   └─ Established >2 years → Bank SME Loan / Government scheme
  │
  ├─ Buy equipment/machinery
  │   └─ Equipment Financing (lowest rate, asset is collateral)
  │
  ├─ Expand business / Open new branch
  │   ├─ Bumiputera → TEKUN / PUNB
  │   ├─ Established good track → Bank loan
  │   └─ Startup/newer business → P2P / Licensed ML
  │
  ├─ Buy property/premises
  │   └─ Commercial property loan (Bank)
  │
  └─ Emergency / urgent need
      ├─ Have asset to collateral → Asset-based lending
      └─ No collateral → Licensed moneylender

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• Want lowest rate → Government schemes (0-4%)
• Want fastest → P2P / Licensed ML (2-7 days)
• Want largest amount → Bank (up to RM10M)
• Want Shariah → Islamic banking
• Want no collateral → P2P / Microfinance
• Bad credit history → Licensed ML / Guarantor loan
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```
